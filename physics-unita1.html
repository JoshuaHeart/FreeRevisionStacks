A.1 Kinematics

The study of motion without considering its causes. Essential foundations for IB Physics HL.

1. Distance vs. Displacement

Distance (Scalar)

The total length of the path traveled. Direction does not matter.

Displacement (Vector)

The straight-line change in position from start to end. Direction is critical.

Key Example: The Circular Track

If an athlete runs one full lap of a 400m circular track:

  • Distance = 400m
  • Displacement = 0m (since they returned to the start)

Pro Tip

Always specify direction for displacement (e.g., "+15m" or "15m North"). In IB exams, forgetting the sign or direction is a common way to lose an easy mark.

3. SUVAT: Constant Acceleration

These five equations describe any object moving with uniform acceleration in a straight line.

$$v = u + at$$
$$s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$$
$$v^2 = u^2 + 2as$$
$$s = \frac{(u+v)t}{2}$$

Step-by-Step Strategy

  1. Identify the 'S-U-V-A-T': List what you know and what you need.
  2. Check units: Ensure everything is in SI (m, s, m/s).
  3. Choose the "Missing Variable" equation: If you don't have time, use \(v^2 = u^2 + 2as\).
  4. Signs: If a ball is thrown up, \(u\) is positive, but acceleration (\(g\)) is \(-9.81 \, m/s^2\).
