Kinematics

Foundational motion analysis for the IB Physics HL Syllabus (2025/2026).

1. Core Definitions

Distance ($d$): Scalar. Total path length traveled.

Displacement ($s$): Vector. Straight-line change in position from start to end point.

Speed ($v$): Scalar. Rate of change of distance.

Velocity ($v$): Vector. Rate of change of displacement.

Key Example: The Circular Track

If an athlete runs exactly one lap of a 400m track: Distance = 400m, but Displacement = 0m.

3. SUVAT: Constant Acceleration

Click an equation to reveal its derivation.

\(v = u + at\) View Derivation

From the definition of acceleration: \(a = \frac{\Delta v}{t} = \frac{v - u}{t}\)

Rearranging: \(at = v - u \implies \mathbf{v = u + at}\)

\(s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2\) View Derivation

Distance is the area under a v-t graph: \(s = \frac{u+v}{2} \cdot t\)

Substitute \(v = u + at\): \(s = \frac{u + (u + at)}{2} \cdot t\)

\(s = \frac{2u + at}{2} \cdot t \implies \mathbf{s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2}\)

\(v^2 = u^2 + 2as\) View Derivation

Eliminate \(t\) by substituting \(t = \frac{v-u}{a}\) into \(s = \frac{u+v}{2} \cdot t\).

\(s = \frac{(v+u)(v-u)}{2a} = \frac{v^2 - u^2}{2a}\)

Rearranging gives: \(\mathbf{v^2 = u^2 + 2as}\)

SUVAT Tips

  • Always define your positive direction (usually Up or Right).
  • If an object is "dropped", \(u = 0\).
  • At maximum height, \(v = 0\).

4. Projectile Motion

Treat vertical and horizontal motion as completely independent.

Horizontal (\(x\))

\(a = 0\) (Constant velocity)

\(u_x = u \cos\theta\)

Vertical (\(y\))

\(a = -9.81 \, m/s^2\)

\(u_y = u \sin\theta\)

5. Fluid Resistance & Terminal Velocity

As an object's speed increases, the resistive force (drag) also increases. Terminal velocity occurs when Drag = Weight.
